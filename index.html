About

Luke Child (he/him) is a PhD Student, Lecturer and Creative Technologist. Luke is a White male with a bushy beard, quiffed short hair and glasses. He usually dons something colourful and abstract, with fashionable items ranging from neon cat Lucy and Yaks to a collection of t-shirts with author John Green's mustaschio-ed face on it.

Originally from Worcester, home of the famous mispronounced sauce, he now operates out of Bristol, UK. Luke is working towards a PhD in Accessible Sonic Interaction Design in Virtual Environments and collaborates with undergraduate students working on research methods, audio programming, and accessible design.

A big fan of weird and wonderful things, Luke likes music that ventures into experimental territories (particularly rhythmic), patching together unconventional signal chains, and making and breaking things in the quest for surprising outcomes. He is almost always listening to Biffy Clyro, or at least music with some weird time-based tomfoolery. He also very much loves Corgis and befriends them at a moments notice. You can also add enjoying Japanese food and whiskey, kicking ass at Scrabble, and having a tense relationship with seagulls, to this list.

Research and Education

PhD

I am currently studying towards a PhD at the University of the West of England. My PhD is currently titled 'Accessible Sonic Interaction Design for Virtual Environments' and is supervised by Dr Natanya Ford and Dr Thomas Mitchell. We are investigating inaccessibility in contemporary video game mechanics, narratives and navigation and researching novel methods of improving access through sonic interaction design.

Lecturing

My teaching is split between three primary areas: first year audio programming, second year research methods, and third year dissertation supervision. I strive to create collaborative and dialogic partnerships with my students, endeavouring to learn just as much alongside them. We also love bringing along a sprinkle of seredipitous creativity, wonder and weirdness in these parts! I encourage students to consider accessibility and inclusive design in their projects, regardless of topic. It is my hope that new graduates are aware that designing for all is an awesome thing to do and bring this ethos forward with them in their work!

Masters

My Masters was in Creative Technologies at the University of the West of England. My thesis was under the supervision of Professor Dan Buzzo and Dr Natanya Ford and was titled 'Utilising elicitation methods to understand and design informative and accessible experiences of digital reverberation'.
