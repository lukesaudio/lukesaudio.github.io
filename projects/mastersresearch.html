During my Master's degree, I extended my passions for reverberation into the realms of elicitation, accessibility and experience.

My dissertation is titled 'Utilising Elicitation Methods to Understand and Design Informative and Accessible Experiences of Digital Reverberation' and focuses on using elicitation methods such as triadic elicitation and repertory grids, as well as open-question surveys to begin to understand how listeners experience reverberation personally. I wanted to consider how individual experiences could be used to inform future implementations or descriptions of reverberation in digital media.
