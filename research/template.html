Making sense of “MWAARP” and “DUUUMM”

A Thematic Analysis of Dialogic Discussions on Accessible Sonic Interaction Design for Video Games

Abstract

Despite ever-increasing numbers of Disabled folks playing video games, the industry seldom sees games that are deemed fully accessible. Video games are pretty complex to design, and so it is often impractical, impossible (and unhelpful) to try and build all-encompassing blanket solutions that provides universal accessibility. Wouldn't THAT be a dream? Instead, we might look to explore accessible design on a localised scale through interrogating the human experience. Tackling different accessibility barriers with specificity and seeing how our solutions may fit into a broader accessibility and inclusive design landscape. In this research project, I wanted to investigate sight motivated gameplay barriers through a sonic interaction lens. I use a method called thematic analysis to analyse discussions I had with nine gamers about their experiences with accessibility barriers in video games. We then contextualise these barriers in sound design and discuss about how intervention with sonic interaction design may provide accessible and inaccessible gameplay experiences. From this, I generated four themes and present to you an exploration of these discussions alongside existing literature to provide recommendations and design scenarios for exploration when developing new accessible video games.
