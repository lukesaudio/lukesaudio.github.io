Research

What do I research?

The primary topic of my research is making games accessible with sound. I have a particular fondness for auditory navigation; "how do we get from A to B using sound and a controller?". I think there's an innate sense of curiousity and bewilderment encoded into space, and games facilitate us being able to interact with those spaces in otherworldly ways. Like, in what other career path can you shoot lightning beams at unsuspecting foes from the back of a dragon flying through the sky, and in the next breath, jump to hyperspace in an alien spaceship and weave betweens asteroids and planets?

The cardinal way of functionally engaging with these spaces though, is through looking at them. Spotting the next vehicle in the distance to swerve around. Identifying the treasure chest on an island out to sea. Seeing that group of angry bandits waiting outside the castle walls. Each of these events though, has a unique sonic identity, and many of us have the ability to place that event in theoretical space with our hearing. So where's the line? Where can we stop functionally looking at our environments, and where can we start functionally listening?
