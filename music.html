Luke makes music?

Indeed, I do. A purveyor of many genres, I began playing guitar aged 12, fell in love with emo and metal in my teens, transitioned to angsty acoustic and niche things in my late teens, and more recently got sprinkled a little math and jazz into my musical palate. Nowadays, I talk about Biffy Clyro all the time, and ya better get used to it. Here, you'll find everything from trippy intergalactic hip hop, to sub two-minute punk with a fellow PhD student, to silly drunken acoustic singalongs as a two piece. Do savour at your own peril.

Below, you'll find a list of musical outputs I have created over the span of my musical lifetime. Links will take you to external sites where my music is hosted, usually Spotify, but occasionally elsewhere.

    Releases

  • Cosmic - Interagalactic trip hop meets mathemathical rap delight

    Cosmic is the intergalactic alter ego of a Midlander lost in the urban landscapes of the west country. He's brewing up transcendental trip hop with alien ambiences and jazz drenched in polyrhythmic delight. He's got the biscuits, and you're invited in for the best cuppa this side of the galaxy.

    • Touchdown - LP - Released 2021 via Satori Records
    • Touchdown - LP - Released 2019 via Satori Records

  • Scrub - two academics explore their coffee addictions through shouty punk

    Two PhD punks making noisy DIY bops from either end of the M4 motorway. What started as a university assessment recording of No Scrubs by TLC, Scrub was born from basement jam sessions, a Rancid CD, and one summer of internship induced sofa-surfing. What proceeded was fast and furious stories about dancing old ladies, drag racing mothers, shit presidents, and loving the ones we have lost.

    • Drag Car Racer - EP - Released 2023 via Flat 10 Records

  • Walking Billboards - Drunken acoustic anthems using random word generators

    Walking Billboards are a Bristol based duo formed of Luke () and Archie. Their common passion for music and songwriting has led them to combine their talents for the good of all humanity. With songs fuelled by the fires of non-sensical drunken lyrical alchemy, we are not to be missed. Or to be missed. You do you, boo.
